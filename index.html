What is Cloud Computing?

Cloud computing is the delivery of computing services, such as storage, servers, databases, networking, and software, over the internet. Instead of managing and maintaining physical data centers, companies use major cloud service providers such as AWS, Azure, and Google Cloud to access essential resources on demand and only pay for what they actually use efficiently and effectively.

The main service models are IaaS (Infrastructure as a Service), PaaS (Platform as a Service), and SaaS (Software as a Service). Data security, backup, and recovery are shared responsibilities between the provider and the user. These models offer various levels of control, customization, and management, enabling businesses to choose the best fit for their needs.
